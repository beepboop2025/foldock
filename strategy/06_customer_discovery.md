# Customer Discovery — validating the B2B thesis

*Goal: turn your ICP from a hypothesis into evidence. Get 3–5 real conversations with labs/biotechs that do structure-based work. Outcome you want: signal ("yes I'd use this") or a pivot — and ideally 1 design partner.*

---

## The one rule: this is NOT a pitch

Run these as **discovery**, not selling (read *The Mom Test*). You are learning about *their* workflow and pain, not asking if they like your idea. People lie to be nice about ideas; they tell the truth about their own work.

- ❌ "Would you use a validated docking platform?" (invites a polite yes)
- ✅ "Walk me through the last time you ran a structure-based screen — what did that actually look like?"

## Target list (start reachable, not aspirational)

**Most reachable → least:**
1. **Academic structural/comp-bio + geroscience labs** — reply rates are highest. Find via bioRxiv authors on geroscience-docking / senolytic-structure papers; Indian institutes (IISc, inStem, IGIB, CCMB).
2. **Small/mid longevity biotechs** — Gero, Shift Bioscience, Rejuvenate Bio, Cambrian, Loyal, plus seed-stage longevity startups (find via Longevity Biotech Fellowship, ARDD, On Deck Longevity).
3. **Computational chemists at biotechs** generally (not just longevity) — they feel the "un-validated in-house scripts" pain acutely.
4. **Aspirational (later):** Altos, Calico, Retro, NewLimit — pursue once you have warm intros / a design partner logo.

> Build a **20-target list**. Aim for 5 completed calls.

## Outreach line (to get the call)

> Subject: 15 min — how does your lab do structure-based screening?
>
> Hi [Name], I'm researching how ageing-focused labs run structure-based / docking work today — what's painful, what tooling you use. I've built a small validated engine in the space but I'm trying to *learn*, not sell. Could I borrow 15 minutes to hear how your group does this? Happy to share what I've found in return.

## The call script (15–20 min)

**1. Context (2 min)** — "Tell me about your group's work and where structure-based methods fit."

**2. Current workflow (the core — 8 min)**
- "Walk me through the last structure-based screen or docking you ran. What did the steps look like?"
- "What tools did you use — and what did you build yourself vs buy?"
- "How do you decide a docking result is trustworthy? Do you validate poses?"  ← *probes your wedge*
- "What took the most time or was most frustrating?"
- "Do you work with variants / mutant structures? How?"  ← *probes variant-triage*
- "Do you ever worry an anti-ageing hit could be pro-cancer? How do you check?"  ← *probes cancer-safety*

**3. Decision & money (5 min)**
- "If a tool solved [the pain they named], who decides whether to adopt it? You, a PI, procurement?"
- "Do you have budget for software/compute tools, or is it all in-house/grants?"
- "What would a tool have to do for it to be worth paying for?"

**4. Soft close (2 min)**
- "This was incredibly helpful. I've built something that does [the specific thing they said was painful] with built-in validation — would you be open to trying it on a real target of yours and telling me where it breaks?" → **design-partner ask**
- If no: "Who else should I be learning from?" → **referral**

## What to listen for

| 🟢 Green (real signal) | 🔴 Red (rethink) |
|---|---|
| They describe a *specific*, repeated, painful workflow | "We don't really do that / a postdoc handles it once a year" |
| They already wrote their own brittle scripts | They're happy with Schrödinger and have budget for it |
| They volunteer the validation/trust pain unprompted | They don't care whether poses are validated |
| They name a budget or a decision-maker | No budget, pure academic grant, no buying power |
| They say "can I try it?" | Polite enthusiasm, no concrete next step |

## Log every call

Track: lab/company · their workflow · biggest pain · do they validate? · variants? · cancer-safety concern? · who decides · budget · design-partner yes/no · referral. After 5 calls, the pattern tells you if the B2B thesis holds, needs sharpening, or needs a pivot — and gives you real quotes for the deck.

**Success = 5 logged calls + 1 design partner (or a clear, evidence-based pivot).** Either outcome is a win; both beat guessing.
