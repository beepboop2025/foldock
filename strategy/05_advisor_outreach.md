# Scientific Advisor Outreach — closing the credibility gap

*The single highest-ROI move pre-raise. One recognised advisor neutralises the "solo non-PhD in deep biology" objection that otherwise kills deep-bio rounds.*

---

## Why this first

Investors funding computational geroscience need to believe the *science* is sound, not just the code. A named scientific advisor on your deck is the cheapest, fastest way to borrow that credibility. Target: **1–2 advisors named before you pitch.**

## What a *good* advisor looks like (in priority order)

1. **A practising geroscience / structural-biology / comp-chem researcher** — runs or works in a lab doing ageing or structure-based drug work.
2. **Reachable** — mid-career PI, senior postdoc, or biotech scientist beats a Nobel-tier name who never replies. A responsive Tier-2 name >> an unreachable Tier-1 logo.
3. **Credibility transferable to investors** — publications, a known institution, or a longevity-biotech affiliation.
4. **Bonus: India/Asia connection** — aligns with your population-specific thesis and is far more reachable.

## Where to actually find them (do this, don't guess)

- **bioRxiv / PubMed**: search recent papers on "epigenetic clock", "senolytics structure", "telomerase structure", "geroprotector docking". The *corresponding authors* and *first authors* of structure/comp papers are ideal — they already do exactly what your engine does.
- **Indian institutes** (most reachable, on-thesis): IISc Bengaluru, inStem, IGIB Delhi, CCMB Hyderabad, NCBS — find faculty working on ageing, protein structure, or computational biology.
- **Longevity biotech scientists** on LinkedIn (BioAge, Gero, Shift Bio, Retro, etc.) — senior scientists, not CEOs.
- **Longevity community**: Foresight Institute, Longevity Biotech Fellowship, ARDD conference author lists, longevity X/Twitter.

> Make a shortlist of **15 names** across these sources. Expect ~10–20% reply rate → 2–3 conversations → 1 advisor.

## What to offer

- **Equity:** 0.25–1.0% advisory shares, vesting over 24 months (standard FAST agreement terms).
- **Time:** ~2–4 hours/month — a sanity-check call + occasional review. Keep it light.
- **Named** on deck + materials (with their permission).

## Cold email template

> **Subject:** Validated computational geroscience engine — 15 min for a sanity check?
>
> Dr. [Name],
>
> I've built a working structure-based engine for reverse-ageing drug research — it folds ageing targets, docks approved drugs, and validates every result by redocking against crystal structures (it reproduces rapamycin→FKBP12 at 0.53 Å and navitoclax→Bcl-xL at 0.90 Å). It also includes a longevity-oncology "cancer-safety" filter that flags telomerase-activation-type oncogenic risk.
>
> I'm a [solo founder / lawyer-technologist] taking this toward a fundable computational-geroscience platform, and I'd value 15 minutes of your read on the science — specifically [one specific question tied to their work, e.g. "whether my variant-triage approach holds up for stability mutations like MTHFR C677T"].
>
> If it's useful, I'd love to explore a formal advisory role. Happy to send the technical brief first. Would [day] work for a short call?
>
> [Name] · [link to one-pager or a single figure]

**Why it works:** leads with a *working, validated* result (not an idea), asks for *their expertise* (flattering, low-commitment), names one specific question (shows you read their work), and only *then* mentions advisory.

## Follow-up (if no reply after 5–6 days)

> Dr. [Name] — quick nudge in case this slipped. Even a 10-minute call or a pointer to someone better placed would help. One figure attached: [the redock-validation figure]. Thanks either way.

## Sequence

1. Build the 15-name shortlist (sources above).
2. Personalise the one specific question per person (this is the work — don't skip it).
3. Send in two batches of ~7; iterate the subject line on the second batch.
4. Convert the first warm reply into the advisor; use their name to reach the next.

**Success = one named advisor on the deck.** That alone moves the "team" score from ~5% toward ~50%.
